8.1 Learning Outcomes

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

  • Articulate data quality decisions: Explain WHAT you’re fixing, WHY it matters biologically, and WHAT trade-offs you’re making

  • Implement targeted transformations: Apply appropriate data cleaning techniques to specific issues in your dataset

  • Verify transformations systematically: Check that your code does what you intended both mechanically and biologically

  • Interpret cleaning consequences: Explain how your transformations change what your data represents and what analyses are possible

  • Document decisions transparently: Create clear records of your reasoning for future reference

  • Evaluate cleaning approaches critically: Review code and provide constructive feedback on data quality decisions

8.2 AI Use Policy for Week 2

ImportantAI Tools (ChatGPT, Claude, GitHub Copilot, etc.) Are NOT Permitted in Week 2

Why we’re restricting AI use at this stage:

  1. Decision-making is the skill, not code generation

Data cleaning requires judgment about:

- What constitutes a "problem" in your specific context

- Which fix preserves biological meaning

- What trade-offs are acceptable

- What assumptions you're making

AI can generate code, but it cannot make these decisions for you. If you use AI now, you’ll outsource the thinking - which is the entire point of this exercise.

  1. Understanding consequences requires deep engagement

When you write code yourself (even slowly, with errors), you:

- Think through each step

- Understand what each line does

- Notice when something unexpected happens

- Learn to debug your own thinking

When AI writes code for you:

- You get a solution but not understanding

- Errors are invisible until analysis fails later

- You can't explain your own work

- You can't adapt the approach to new situations
  1. This is your chance to build foundational skills

Week Two is about developing your mental model of data transformation:

- What happens when I remove NAs?

- How does standardization change my data?

- What information am I losing or preserving?

8.3 Session Overview

  1. Compare your Week 2 plans with a partner (20 min)

  2. Implement your top 2 cleaning fixes (20 min)

  3. Review each other’s code (20 min)

  4. Implement your partner’s code (20 min)

  5. Check your project is well organised and code is organised and commented (20 min)

8.3.1 What you need

  • Your completed between-session work (tutorials + checklists + plan)

  • Questions you identified in your plan

  • Commitment to do your own thinking and coding
